Lost Idyll

2015

Digital video

00:11:55 loop


Alternately one or two images projected side by side, this video installation shows a woman transporting a cumbersome scaffolding through an autumnal forest and a no man’s land. Entering the field of one projection and going out of the other, this woman makes a Sisyphean movement, repeating from a place to another in a continuous mobility.

Ephemeral structures composing our urban landscape, the scaffolding characterize the spectacle of the everyday life and is a common figure of our collective imaginary. By pulling this structure with the strength of her arms, this woman, whom no detail distinguishes, mediates the relationship that every individual maintains with a coercive environment.









The powerful wind and the restless sky confer a dramatic dimension to the diptych, whereas the slowness of the sequences immerses the viewer into contemplation. At times, the projected sequences are identical but slightly delayed, evoking a double consciousness, maybe confused, which seems to derive.



Installation at Chapelle des Carmélites during the Rencontres Traverse Vidéo, Toulouse.

Lost Idyll

2015

Digital video

00:11:32 loop


This video follows a woman who transports her enormous and cumbersome scaffolding on wheels across wide fields and ploughed lands.

This gesture of metaphoric accent is repeated from a place to another in a continuous mobility. The scaffolding, real structure of temporary constructions in the urban landscapes, characterize the spectacle of the everyday which surrounds us in a collective imaginary. The movement of the referent from a singular space to another decontextualize the narrative and make us fall over in the metaphor, whereas the landscape and the scaffolding oppose each other. By pulling, she transports at the same time her self identity and a collective imaginary that recalls to a structure to which the being is subjected and joins in a tension between the individual, the collective and the fictitious imaginaries.









The wind and the lighting filtered by the movement of the clouds amplifies this tension, while the slowness of the sequences brings the viewer in contemplation.

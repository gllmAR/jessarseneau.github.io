Lost Idyll

2015

Digital video

00:11:32 loop


This video follows a woman who transports her enormous and cumbersome scaffolding on wheels through uncluttered fields and ploughed lands.

This gesture of metaphoric accent is repeating from a place to another in a continuous mobility. The scaffolding, real structure temporary constructions in the urban landscapes, characterize the spectacle of the everyday which surrounds us. By pulling her scaffolding, she transports at the same time her self identity and a collective memory that recalls to a structure which we are subjected to and joins in a tension with the reciprocal transformations between the individual, the collective and the fictitious imaginary.









The wind and the lighting filtered by the movement of the clouds amplifies this tension, while the slowness of the sequences brings the viewer in contemplation and waiting. Movement of the referent from a singular space to another decontextualize the narrative and make us fall over to the metaphor, whereas the landscape and the scaffolding opposes each other.

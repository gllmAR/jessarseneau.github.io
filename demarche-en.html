About


Jessica Arseneau is an Acadian artist using performance, video, drawing and installation. Her work addresses the collective imaginary and systems of representation where she explores events, rituals and gestures. The resulted experiences and environments suggest a suspended narrative — an implausible storyline existing in a temporal state between successive created actions of drama and those who have already taken place.

Arseneau received her B.A. from the Université de Moncton (2011) and was a member of the cooperative artist studio Atelier King Kong from 2013 to 2015 (Montreal). Public presentations of her work occurred at art spaces such as Darling Foundry (Montreal), BronxArtSpace (New York), FICFA (Moncton) and Janaklees for Visual Arts (Alexandrie), Traverse Vidéo (Toulouse), HGB Galerie (Leipzig), Agora Collective (Berlin), Gallery 301 (Seoul). She currently studies in HGB Leipzig, Germany.

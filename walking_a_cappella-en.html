Walking A Cappella

2014

Audiovisual installation

Audio 00:10:00 loop

Video 00:07:00 loop




Walking A Cappella is an audiovisual installation with a soundscape of feminine voices and a circular video projection on the floor. During an artist in residency program at ElMadina for Performing and Digital Arts (Alexandria, 2014), I have met several women and recorded their singing to amplify their voices. They were free to sing anything they wanted. The orchestration of the recordings reveals melodies that are part of the cultural field that frames the atmosphere of West Asia and North Africa. With the layering and the repetition of the sounds, I have made a composition that makes a socio-polical shout deployed by singing women, thus, reflecting the complexity of their current situation. The video shows images in Alexandria, among others, an old theater’s ruin. While the sound plays for ten minutes on a loop, the video repeats itself after seven minutes, making asynchronous combinations that recall to a disrupted situation, an echo to the Egyptian Revolution of 2011. The project includes the voices of Dalia Galal, Nahla Hassan, Yasmine Hegazy, Kholoud Issa, Sondos Muhammad, Dalia Samer, Amira Samir and Tuqa Zeidan.

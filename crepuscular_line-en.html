Crepuscular Line, 2023

Video HD, sound stereo

7:45 min.



Featuring: Aurélien Finance

Sculptor: Philippe Leonard

Project Assistant: Laura Haby

Camera: Jessica Arseneau, Laura Haby

Video/audio/colour editing: Jessica Arseneau



A stone carved in the shape of a human face that seems to be asleep is laying in a forest. The formation of a rock dating from distant geological eras, this stone could be asleep for several thousand years or be permanently tired, with moss growing in the hollows of the rings under its eyes. A human figure lies on top of the sculpture with his arms wrapped around it and staying over the sleeping head. Satellite images of the earth scroll across the screen along the twilight zone, showing both night and day, dawn and dusk. These images capture the permanently illuminated cities, showing the Earth as an almost always lit site that allows us to continue restlessly. However, some locations are not lit at night, such as some places in crisis and areas that appear untouched. While the tiredness that emerges from the video may be related to the constant activity driven by Western societies, it also reflects a broader sense of loneliness and longing—both on a micro and a macro level.
